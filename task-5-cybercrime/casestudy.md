Part A – Case Study (Phishing Attack) 
Title: A College Student Falls Victim to Phishing 
Riya, a second-year college student, received an email that appeared to be from her 
university’s administration. The message claimed there was an urgent issue with her 
student portal and asked her to “verify her account” by clicking a link. The email looked 
genuine, including the university logo and official language. 
Step-by-step, the attack unfolded like this: 
1. The attacker sent a fake email mimicking a trusted institution.  
2. Riya clicked the link, which redirected her to a fake login page.  
3. She entered her username and password.  
4. The attacker captured her credentials instantly.  
5. The hacker used her account to access personal data and send similar phishing 
emails to her contacts.  
Phishing typically targets students, employees, and anyone who frequently uses email 
or online services. Young users are especially vulnerable due to lack of awareness and 
urgency in responding to messages. 
The consequences can be serious. Riya lost access to her account temporarily, and her 
personal data—including emails and saved documents—was compromised. In some 
cases, attackers may use stolen credentials for financial fraud or identity theft. 
Additionally, her contacts were exposed to similar scams, spreading the threat further. 
This case highlights how a simple click can lead to major digital security risks.
